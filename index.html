Hypergeometric Distribution

What is it?

In probability theory and statistics, the hypergeometric distribution is a discrete probability distribution that describes the probability of k successes (random draws for which the object drawn has a specified feature) in n draws, without replacement, from a finite population of size N that contains exactly K objects with that feature, wherein each draw is either a success or a failure.

Hypergeometric vs Binomial Distribution

The binomial distribution applies to scenarios with a fixed number of independent trials conducted with replacement, where each trial has a constant probability of success, such as flipping a coin multiple times. In contrast, the hypergeometric distribution applies to scenarios with a fixed number of dependent trials conducted without replacement, where the probability of success changes as the finite population decreases with each draw, such as drawing cards from a deck without replacement. The key difference is that in the binomial distribution, trials are independent and the success probability remains constant, whereas in the hypergeometric distribution, trials are dependent and the success probability varies.

Formula

The following conditions characterize the hypergeometric distribution:

  • The result of each draw (the elements of the population being sampled) can be classified into one of two mutually exclusive categories (e.g. Pass/Fail or Employed/Unemployed).
  • The probability of a success changes on each draw, as each draw decreases the population (sampling without replacement from a finite population).
